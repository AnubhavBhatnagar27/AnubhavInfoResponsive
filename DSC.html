Digital Signature Certificates (DSCs) are an essential part of India's digital landscape, providing a secure and reliable means of authentication for electronic transactions. Issued by authorized Certifying Authorities (CAs) under the Information Technology Act, 2000, DSCs play a vital role in establishing the identity of individuals and organizations in the digital realm. By utilizing mathematical algorithms and public key infrastructure (PKI) technology, DSCs ensure the authenticity, integrity, and non-repudiation of digital documents, messages, and transactions. With their legal recognition and widespread adoption, DSCs have become instrumental in facilitating trustworthy and efficient digital interactions in various sectors, contributing to the growth of India's digital economy.
